Projects

Food Craving Survey

This was a fun project for me, targeted at implementing a survey system for images from the ground up. Visitors to the website are asked to rate different types of food using a range slider. An additional download functionality enables looking back at responses of users from the past. The app is deployed on Heroku and connected to a remote PostgreSQL database for storage.

E Train Stations

I applied for the Alexa Student Developer Diploma with this Alexa skill. The app is for people who ride the E Train in the NYC Subway. Through a series of random questions, players test their knowledge about the E Train stations. After deploying the skill on the Amazon store, I was selected for the Developer Diploma, along with some Amazon Echo goodies. This was my first deployment of any sort and I'm quite proud of that.

Rent-Buy-Sell

I was part of a team of friends tasked with implementing a pseudo-Ebay web application, using any framework/tool of choice. We stuck with Django and a SQLite database, with the prime focus on the user stories of buying, selling, and renting virtual items on a make-believe marketplace. Along with a search functionality and basic templating of products and fields, the project provided on the total experience of a transaction from selecting products to checkout.

PyStock

I worked with a team to provide a Python implementation of different types of transactions in a stock exchange scenario, specifically buying on margin and short selling. The MySQL database was queried using a Python driver and the data gathered was analyzed and then displayed on a HTML website using Python classes.
